# Riley's_Office Brand Guide

## Brand position
Riley's_Office is the operator HQ for OpenClaw: visually distinctive, operationally serious, and built for fast intervention from iPhone.

## Brand promise
Walk into a live headquarters, understand what matters immediately, and act safely without losing governed execution context.

## Tone
- calm under pressure
- high-agency
- clear over clever
- premium, not playful
- cinematic enough to feel spatial, restrained enough to stay credible

## Visual direction
- modern office / war-room instead of abstract sci-fi skyline
- strong sense of rooms, desks, circulation, glazing, displays, and work surfaces
- mobile-first composition with immediate hierarchy
- motion that suggests movement through space, never game noise

## UI principles
1. **Glanceable first** - status, blockers, and active work should read in seconds.
2. **Spatial but useful** - every office, room, and desk metaphor must map to real runtime context.
3. **Governed by default** - actions should feel deliberate, permission-aware, and reviewable.
4. **Premium clarity** - polished materials, contrast, and typography without clutter.
5. **Fallback dignity** - non-WebGL and reduced-motion views must still communicate the office story.

## Color / atmosphere guidance
- deep graphite and slate foundations
- cool glass blues for environment depth
- warm amber accents for active work and lighting
- high-contrast status colors for health, alerts, blockers, and approvals
- avoid neon overload and arcade palettes

## Content style
- short, operator-grade labels
- direct task language
- evidence-forward status copy
- minimal filler or marketing adjectives

## Experience keywords
- headquarters
- command center
- office floor
- war room
- desks
- evidence
- live state
- governed action

## Anti-patterns
- cartoon office metaphors
- noisy cyberpunk styling
- decorative 3D that obscures status
- dense enterprise dashboard tables as the primary experience
- vague status language without evidence or ownership
